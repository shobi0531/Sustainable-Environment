AIR POLLUTION

Air pollution is the presence of substances in the atmosphere that can be harmful to living organisms or the environment. These substances, known as pollutants, can come from natural sources or human activities. Common air pollutants include:

  • Particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10)
  • Nitrogen oxides (NOx)
  • Sulfur dioxide (SO2)
  • Carbon monoxide (CO)
  • Volatile organic compounds (VOCs)
  • Ground-level ozone (O3)

Air pollution can have serious health effects on humans, including respiratory problems, heart disease, and even premature death. It can also harm the environment, leading to issues like acid rain, smog, and damage to ecosystems.
